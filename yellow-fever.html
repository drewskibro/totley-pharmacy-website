ABOUT YELLOW FEVER

What is Yellow Fever?

A serious viral infection transmitted by mosquitoes in tropical regions of Africa and South America

Viral Infection

Caused by a virus transmitted through the bite of infected Aedes or Haemagogus mosquitoes, primarily found in tropical regions of Africa and South America.

Serious Symptoms

Symptoms include fever, headache, jaundice (yellowing of skin), muscle pain, nausea, and vomiting. Severe cases can cause organ failure.

No Cure, But Preventable

There's no specific treatment for yellow fever, but the vaccine provides excellent protection. A single dose offers lifetime immunity.

47 Risk Countries

Found across sub-Saharan Africa and tropical South America, including popular destinations like Kenya, Brazil, Peru, and Colombia.

Important: Vaccination Required for Entry

MANDATORY

VACCINATION DETAILS

What to expect at your appointment

Simple, straightforward vaccination process at our Sheffield clinic

Single Dose Vaccination

Only one injection needed for lifetime protection. No booster doses required for most travellers.

Book 10 Days Before Travel

Vaccine must be given at least 10 days before travel to allow immunity to develop. Certificate becomes valid after 10 days.

Mild Side Effects

Usually mild: soreness at injection site, headache, or low-grade fever for 1-2 days. Most people feel fine.

Lifetime Certificate Validity

Valid for life under WHO guidelines. However, some countries may have specific requirements—we'll advise based on your destination.

Age 9 Months & Older

Suitable for travellers aged 9 months and older. Not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding.
