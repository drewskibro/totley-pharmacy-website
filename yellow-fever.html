ABOUT YELLOW FEVER

What is Yellow Fever?

A serious viral infection transmitted by mosquitoes in tropical regions of Africa and South America

Viral Infection

Caused by a virus transmitted through the bite of infected Aedes or Haemagogus mosquitoes, primarily found in tropical regions of Africa and South America.

Serious Symptoms

Symptoms include fever, headache, jaundice (yellowing of skin), muscle pain, nausea, and vomiting. Severe cases can cause organ failure.

No Cure, But Preventable

There's no specific treatment for yellow fever, but the vaccine provides excellent protection. A single dose offers lifetime immunity.

47 Risk Countries

Found across sub-Saharan Africa and tropical South America, including popular destinations like Kenya, Brazil, Peru, and Colombia.

Important: Vaccination Required for Entry

Many countries require proof of yellow fever vaccination for entry. As an official Yellow Fever Centre, we provide internationally recognised certificates on the day of vaccination.

VACCINATION DETAILS

What to expect

Everything you need to know about getting your yellow fever vaccination

Single Dose

Only one injection needed for lifetime protection. No booster doses required.

10 Days Before Travel

Vaccine must be given at least 10 days before travel to allow immunity to develop.

Lifetime Protection

Valid for life. Certificate never expires and is accepted worldwide.

Age Requirements

Suitable for travellers aged 9 months and older. Not recommended during pregnancy.

Side Effects

Usually mild: soreness at injection site, headache, or low-grade fever for 1-2 days.

Private Only

Yellow fever vaccination is not available on the NHS. Private fee applies.

CONTRAINDICATIONS

Who cannot have the vaccine?

Yellow fever vaccine is not suitable for everyone. We'll assess your eligibility during consultation.

Not Suitable For:

Pregnant women
Breastfeeding mothers (first 6 months)
Infants under 9 months
Severe egg allergy
Weakened immune system
Thymus gland disorders
Age 60+ (requires assessment)
Recent live vaccines (within 4 weeks)

Medical Exemption Certificates Available

If you cannot have the vaccine for medical reasons, we can provide a Medical Exemption Certificate for travel purposes.
