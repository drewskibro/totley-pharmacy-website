UNDERSTANDING COVID BOOSTERS

Understanding COVID Boosters - Sheffield Guide

Why COVID Boosters Matter:

COVID-19 continues to circulate in Sheffield and across the UK. While most people now have some immunity from previous infections or vaccinations, booster doses strengthen your protection, particularly against severe illness.

How COVID Boosters Work:

Your Immune System Needs Reminding: Immunity from COVID vaccines gradually decreases over time. A booster dose "reminds" your immune system how to fight COVID-19, strengthening your protection.

1

Immunity Boost (Days 1-14)

Your immune system rapidly responds to the booster, producing antibodies and memory cells that recognize COVID-19.

3

Enhanced Protection (Months)

You have strengthened protection against severe COVID-19 illness. Protection is particularly strong against hospitalization and serious complications.

Protection from COVID boosters is strongest in the first few months and gradually decreases over time. Many Sheffield residents choose to get boosters every 6-12 months for ongoing protection.

SIDE EFFECTS & SAFETY

COVID Booster Side Effects & Safety

Common Side Effects from Sheffield COVID Boosters (Normal & Expected)

At Injection Site:

  • Soreness where Sheffield COVID jab was given
  • Redness or swelling
  • Usually resolves within 1-2 days

General:

  • Headache after Sheffield COVID vaccination
  • Tiredness or fatigue
  • Muscle aches
  • Mild fever or chills
  • Generally feeling unwell
  • Usually resolves within 1-2 days

Important: These side effects show your immune system is responding to the Sheffield COVID booster. They typically resolve quickly.

Managing Side Effects:

  • Rest if needed
  • Drink plenty of fluids
  • Paracetamol can help with fever or discomfort (follow dosage instructions)
  • Most Sheffield residents feel back to normal within 24-48 hours

Serious Side Effects:

Serious side effects from COVID boosters are extremely rare. Sheffield Wellness Clinic has emergency equipment on-site and our pharmacist is trained to manage any reactions.

When to Seek Medical Advice:

Contact us or seek medical attention if you experience:

  • Severe allergic reaction symptoms
  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • Severe persistent headache
  • Unusual symptoms causing concern
