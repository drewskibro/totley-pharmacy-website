ABOUT DIPHTHERIA

What is Diphtheria?

A serious bacterial infection affecting the nose, throat, and airways, still present in many countries worldwide

Bacterial Infection

Caused by the bacterium Corynebacterium diphtheriae, which produces a toxin affecting the respiratory system. Spreads through respiratory droplets from coughing or sneezing.

Respiratory Symptoms

Symptoms include sore throat, fever, swollen neck glands, and a thick grey coating in the throat that can block airways. Can cause heart and nerve damage in severe cases.

Highly Preventable

Diphtheria is rare in the UK thanks to childhood vaccination programmes, but remains common in many countries. Regular boosters maintain your protection when travelling.

Present Worldwide

Diphtheria is still endemic in parts of Africa, Asia, South America, and Eastern Europe. Sheffield travellers to these regions should ensure their booster is up to date.

Important: Check Your Booster Status

ESSENTIAL

Most Sheffield adults received diphtheria vaccination as children, but immunity wanes over time. If it's been more than 10 years since your last booster, you need a top-up before travelling—especially to developing countries. Book a consultation at our Totley clinic to check your status.

VACCINATION DETAILS

What to expect at your Sheffield appointment

Simple, straightforward booster vaccination at our Totley clinic

Single Booster Injection

One injection of the Td/IPV booster provides 10 years of protection against diphtheria, tetanus, and polio. Quick and convenient at our Sheffield clinic.

Book Before Travel

Ideally get your booster 4-6 weeks before travel, though it can be given closer to departure if needed. We'll advise Sheffield travellers on timing.

Minimal Side Effects

Usually mild: soreness at injection site, slight swelling, or low-grade fever for 1-2 days. Most Sheffield patients experience no issues.
