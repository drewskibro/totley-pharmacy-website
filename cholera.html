ABOUT CHOLERA

What is Cholera?

A bacterial infection spread through contaminated water and food in areas with poor sanitation

Bacterial Infection

Caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae, spread through contaminated water and food in areas with inadequate sanitation and water treatment.

Severe Dehydration

Symptoms include severe watery diarrhoea, vomiting, and rapid dehydration. Without treatment, severe cases can be life-threatening within hours.

Treatable & Preventable

Cholera is treatable with rehydration therapy and antibiotics. The oral vaccine provides excellent prevention for travellers to high-risk areas.

High-Risk Regions

Found in parts of Africa, South Asia, and Central/South America where sanitation is poor. Risk increases during humanitarian crises and natural disasters.

Bonus: Traveller's Diarrhoea Protection

ADDED BENEFIT

The cholera vaccine also provides some protection against ETEC, the bacteria responsible for most cases of traveller's diarrhoea. This makes it valuable even for destinations where cholera risk is low.
