Malaria Prevention for India

Malaria Risk Throughout India

Malaria is present in most parts of India, with higher risk in rural and forested areas. Major cities like Delhi and Mumbai have lower risk but it's still present.

Antimalarial Medication Recommended

We strongly recommend antimalarial tablets for most travellers to India. The specific medication depends on your itinerary and the regions you'll visit.

Prevention Measures

Use DEET-based insect repellent, wear long sleeves/trousers at dusk and dawn, sleep under mosquito nets, and stay in air-conditioned or screened accommodation.

Personalised Malaria Assessment

We'll assess your specific itinerary and prescribe the most appropriate antimalarial medication for your trip to India.

HEALTH TIPS FOR INDIA

Stay healthy in India

Essential health advice for your India trip

Only Drink Bottled Water

Never drink tap water or use ice cubes. Only consume sealed bottled water and use it for brushing teeth. Avoid drinks with ice.

Strict Food Safety

Only eat freshly cooked hot food. Avoid street food, raw vegetables, salads, and unpasteurised dairy products.

Mosquito Protection

Use DEET-based repellent, wear long sleeves/trousers at dusk and dawn, and sleep under mosquito nets.

Hand Hygiene

Wash hands frequently with soap and water or use alcohol-based hand sanitiser, especially before eating.

Travel Health Kit

Pack diarrhoea medication, oral rehydration salts, antihistamines, pain relief, and any prescription medications.

Travel Insurance

Ensure comprehensive travel insurance covers medical treatment and emergency evacuation from India.
