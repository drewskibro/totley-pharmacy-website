HEALTH INFORMATION

Malaria and other health risks in India

Malaria risk

Malaria is present throughout India, with varying risk levels. Lower risk in major cities like Delhi and Mumbai, but moderate to high risk in rural areas, particularly in the northeast states, Odisha, and parts of Rajasthan. We'll prescribe appropriate antimalarial medication based on your specific itinerary.

Other considerations

  • Dengue fever is widespread, especially during and after monsoon season
  • Traveller's diarrhoea is very common - strict food and water precautions essential
  • Avoid tap water, ice, and raw foods
  • Air pollution can be severe in major cities
  • Comprehensive travel insurance with medical evacuation cover essential
  • Altitude sickness risk if visiting Himalayan regions

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

India travel health questions

Yellow fever vaccination is only required if you're arriving from a country with yellow fever risk. If you're travelling directly from the UK, it's not required. However, if you're stopping in an endemic country, you'll need the certificate.

Malaria is present throughout India with varying risk levels. Major cities like Delhi and Mumbai have lower risk, but rural areas, especially in the northeast, Odisha, and parts of Rajasthan have moderate to high risk. We'll assess your specific itinerary and prescribe appropriate antimalarial medication.

No, never drink tap water in India. Always use bottled water (check the seal is intact), boiled water, or water purification tablets. Avoid ice in drinks, raw salads, and unpeeled fruits. Traveller's diarrhoea is very common in India.

Children can receive most travel vaccines, though some have minimum age requirements. Hepatitis A can be given from age 1, Typhoid from age 2, and Rabies from any age. We'll advise on age-appropriate protection during your consultation.

Most vaccines provide long-term protection. Hepatitis A lasts 10+ years, Typhoid lasts 3 years, Tetanus lasts 10 years, Japanese Encephalitis lasts up to 2 years, and Rabies pre-exposure lasts 2-3 years. We'll provide a full vaccination record.

Yes, most travel vaccines can be given together during the same visit. However, some vaccines like Japanese Encephalitis and Rabies require multiple doses over several weeks, so early booking is important.

Book an appointment as soon as possible. While 6-8 weeks is ideal, most vaccines still provide good protection even if given shortly before travel. Some protection is always better than none. We can prioritize the most important vaccines for your itinerary.

Yes, comprehensive travel insurance is absolutely essential for India. Medical facilities vary greatly in quality, and good private hospitals are expensive. Ensure your insurance covers medical evacuation, as this may be necessary in serious cases.

If you're visiting high-altitude areas like Ladakh, Manali, or trekking in the Himalayas, altitude sickness is a real risk. We can prescribe medication to help prevent altitude sickness and provide advice on acclimatization.

Air pollution can be severe in major Indian cities, especially Delhi during winter months. If you have respiratory conditions like asthma, bring extra medication and consider wearing a pollution mask. Check air quality forecasts before your trip.
