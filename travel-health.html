FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Travel health questions answered

Get answers to the most common travel health questions

We recommend booking 6-8 weeks before travel. Some vaccines require multiple doses over several weeks, and your body needs time to build immunity. However, we can still help with last-minute travelcontact us as soon as possible.

Yellow fever certificates are required for entry to certain countries in Africa and South America. As an official yellow fever vaccination centre, we can provide the internationally recognised certificate on the day of your vaccination.

Costs vary depending on which vaccines you need. During your consultation, we'll provide a full breakdown of costs with no hidden fees. Some vaccines may be available free on the NHS.

Bring your travel itinerary, any previous vaccination records, and details of any medical conditions or medications you're taking. This helps us provide the most accurate advice for your trip.

This depends on your destination and the time of year you're travelling. We'll assess your malaria risk during consultation and prescribe appropriate antimalarial medication if needed.

Some travel vaccines like hepatitis A, typhoid, and cholera are available free on the NHS. Others, including yellow fever and rabies, are only available privately. We'll advise you on what's available during your consultation.
