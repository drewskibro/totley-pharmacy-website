GOT QUESTIONS?

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our medical weight loss programme.

How much does the weight loss programme cost?
The programme costs £199 per month, which includes your prescription medication (Wegovy or Mounjaro), weekly check-ins with Tajinder, nutrition guidance, and ongoing support. There's also a one-off £49 initial consultation fee. We don't require long-term contracts—you can pause or stop at any time.
Am I suitable for prescription weight loss medication?
You may be suitable if you have a BMI of 30 or above, or 27 or above with a weight-related health condition (such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, or sleep apnoea). Tajinder will assess your full medical history during your free consultation to determine if medication is safe and appropriate for you.
What medications do you prescribe?
We prescribe GLP-1 medications including Wegovy (semaglutide) and Mounjaro (tirzepatide). These are the same medications used in clinical trials showing average weight loss of 15-20% of body weight. Tajinder will recommend the most suitable option based on your health profile, preferences, and budget.
How quickly will I see results?
Most patients notice reduced hunger within the first week. Visible weight loss typically begins in weeks 2-4, with average losses of 1-2lbs per week. By 3 months, most patients have lost 5-10% of their starting weight. Results vary based on starting weight, adherence, and individual factors.
What are the side effects?
Common side effects include nausea, constipation, and reduced appetite, especially in the first few weeks. These usually improve as your body adjusts. We start you on a low dose and increase gradually to minimise side effects. Tajinder provides guidance on managing any discomfort, and you'll have ongoing support throughout your treatment.
Do I need to diet and exercise?
The medication significantly reduces hunger and cravings, making it much easier to eat less naturally. We provide nutrition guidance to help you make sustainable choices, but there's no strict diet plan. Regular movement is encouraged for overall health, but intense exercise isn't required. Many patients find they naturally become more active as they lose weight.
What happens when I stop the medication?
We work with you to develop a sustainable maintenance plan before stopping medication. Some patients continue on a lower maintenance dose, whilst others successfully transition to lifestyle changes alone. The habits and skills you've built during the programme help you maintain your results long-term.
How long will I need to take the medication?
Most patients stay on medication for 6-18 months to reach their goal weight. Some continue longer for maintenance. There's no fixed timeline—we review your progress regularly and adjust your plan based on your goals, results, and preferences. You're in control of your journey.
